<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Progression Route">

If you are new to **legends battlegrounds kade**, following a structured training path is more effective than jumping straight into public servers. Use the following steps to build muscle memory and understand the flow of a standard match.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Neutral Movement Training">
    Enter a private server and practice side-dashing around stationary targets. Your goal is to land an M1 string by dashing behind the opponent's guard. Practice this until you can consistently hit three M1s without the target blocking.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The 3-M1 Rule">
    In combat, never finish a full 4-M1 chain unless you want to knock the opponent away. Instead, stop at the third hit and immediately transition into an **Uppercut** (to launch) or a **Downslam** (to test their evasive timing).
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Evasive Baiting">
    Engage an opponent and land a Downslam. Do not follow up immediately. Watch if they use their **Q evasive**. If they do, block their retaliation and then commit your full cooldowns while their escape is on recharge.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Awakening Confirmation">
    Only activate your **G transformation** after a successful knockback. This prevents the opponent from hitting you during the startup frames. Once awakened, prioritize your unblockable skills to secure the elimination.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="Wall Combo Trick">
When fighting near a wall, use a side dash to angle your knockback toward the geometry. This triggers the **Wall Combo** animation, which provides free damage and resets your positioning for an aerial follow-up.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Advanced Movesets and Variants">

The depth of **legends battlegrounds kade** lies in the hidden variants of standard moves. Certain characters, particularly Jun, can modify their skills based on the environment or previous inputs. Understanding these interactions is what separates casual players from the "Kade" elite.

**Martial Legend Moveset Overview:**
Martial Legend relies on the **Draconic Instinct** passive, which triggers after a successful counter. This increases movement speed, allowing for faster follow-ups after a defensive play.

| Skill Name | Role | Properties | Best Use |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Dragon Rush** | Chase | Forward grab, blockable | Catching falling targets |
| **Tiger Palm** | Guard Break | Unblockable, hits ragdoll | Ending combos on blockers |
| **Panther Combo** | Extender | Multi-hit, launching kick | Connecting after an uppercut |
| **Triple Kick** | Counter | Stance, punishes attacks | Reading predictable M1 strings |

**Jun's Variant Mechanics:**
Jun is unique because his **Virgule Cascade** (Input 4) changes based on whether the target is on the ground, in the air, or if it is used during **Glimpse of Power**.

| Variant Name | Trigger Condition | Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Airborne Virgule** | Use 4 after an Uppercut | Aerial slash sequence |
| **Glimpse Cascade** | Use 4 during Glimpse (1) | Combined martial-arts sequence |
| **Wall Variant** | Use 4 after a Wall Launch | Pins target for extra damage |

<Callout type="warning" title="Execution Risk">
While **Glimpse Cascade** deals massive damage, missing the initial **Glimpse of Power** hit will put both skills on cooldown. Only attempt this variant when the opponent is stunned or has no evasive.
</Callout>
