<Section icon={Shield} title="Core Combat Loop and Beginner Progression Guide">

Before attempting high-damage combos, you must master the fundamental mechanics of movement, defense, and resource management. The core combat loop revolves around alternating light attacks (M1), dashing to reposition, blocking incoming strings, and managing your evasive meter.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Select Your Starting Fighter">
    Begin your journey with Martial Legend. His kit is highly balanced, featuring a direct counter and clear combo extenders that teach you the timing of launchers and knockbacks without overwhelming you with complex variants.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Master the Official Controls">
    Familiarize yourself with the default keybinds. Press **Left Click** for basic M1 chains, **Q** to dash (or escape ragdolls), **F** to block incoming strikes, **G** to trigger your transformation when the awakening meter is full, and **B** to open the cosmetic emote wheel.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Practice Defensive Spacing">
    Avoid forward-dashing directly into an opponent's block. Instead, use side dashes to angle around their guard, circle at medium range, and wait for them to miss an ability before starting your own light attack chain.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Manage Your Q Evasive Smartly">
    Using your Q dash while ragdolled is your only escape tool, but it has a lengthy cooldown. Only use it when the opponent still has powerful special moves available to extend their combo; otherwise, take the knockdown and reset.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The table below outlines the universal input controls and their primary tactical applications in battle.

| Input | Action | Tactical Purpose | Recovery Risk |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Left Click** | Light Attack (M1) | Starter, pressure, and combo filler | Low recovery on hit |
| **Q Key** | Dash / Evasive | Repositioning, catching landings, ragdoll escape | High cooldown if used as escape |
| **F Key** | Block | Mitigates standard M1 and blockable special damage | Vulnerable to guard breaks |
| **G Key** | Awakening | Accesses ultimate movesets and creates space | Long charge-up required |
| **B Key** | Emote Wheel | Custom social animations and celebrations | Locks movement temporarily |

<Callout type="warning" title="Critical Warning">
Wasting your Q evasive early in a round is the fastest way to lose a match. Experienced players will actively bait your escape using basic downslams, only to unleash their maximum-damage combo once your dash is on cooldown.
</Callout>

Shinji relies heavily on his **Lightning Rage** passive. When Flashstep Fury is on cooldown, landing Nunchuck Flurry or Body Flicker refreshes it, allowing for extended pressure loops.

| Move Name | Input Key | Guard Interaction | Primary Combat Role |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Nunchuck Flurry** | 1 | Blockable | Rising launcher, triggers passive reset |
| **Body Flicker** | 2 | Blockable | Directional dash, multi-target pressure |
| **Lightning Flash** | 3 | Bypasses Block | Main guard-breaker, launches target |
| **Flashstep Fury** | 4 | Blockable | Rapid electric barrage, high AoE damage |

Martial Legend features a highly balanced kit built around direct launchers and a powerful defensive counter.
