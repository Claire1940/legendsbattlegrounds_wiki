<Section icon={Map} title="Fundamentals of Combo Execution">

In the high-speed arena of Legends Battlegrounds, victory is determined by how efficiently you can convert a single opening into a high-damage sequence. The combat system favors players who understand the "neutral game"—the period where both fighters are looking for a hit—and can transition into **legends battlegrounds best combos** without mashing buttons. The most important rule for any beginner is the M1 limit. While a full light-attack chain knocks the opponent away, stopping at three hits allows you to follow up with an Uppercut (to launch) or a Downslam (to ground).

Understanding these starters is the first step toward competitive play. You must also account for the opponent's defensive options. Every player has access to an "Evasive" or ragdoll cancel by pressing Q while being hit. If you spend all your skills while the opponent still has their Evasive ready, they will simply teleport away, leaving you with empty cooldowns and no defense.

| Starter Type | Input Sequence | Primary Purpose | Best Follow-up |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Uppercut Starter** | 3 M1 + Jump/Space | Launches target into the air | Airborne skills or Panther Combo |
| **Downslam Starter** | 3 M1 + Ground Aim | Pins target to the floor | Ground-hitting skills or Tiger Palm |
| **Dash Starter** | Forward Dash + M1 | Closes distance and initiates hitstun | Full M1 string or skill confirm |
| **Side-Dash Bait** | Q (Side) + M1 | Bypasses blocks by hitting the flank | Immediate Uppercut launcher |

<Callout type="tip" title="Pro Timing Tip">
Wait for the opponent to spend their Q (Evasive) before using your longest cinematic skills. A "spent" opponent is forced to take the full damage of your most powerful strings.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Jun: Technical Mastery and Variants">

Jun is widely considered the highest-skill character in Legends Battlegrounds due to his "Variant" system. Unlike other fighters who have static skills, Jun can combine his abilities to create entirely new animations and damage profiles. For example, triggering Virgule Cascade (Skill 4) during the animation of Glimpse of Power (Skill 1) results in the "Glimpse Cascade," a devastating martial arts sequence that deals significantly more damage than the two skills used separately.

| Variant Name | Required Input | Effect / Change |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Glimpse Cascade** | Skill 1, then Skill 4 during rush | Unique dagger and kick sequence |
| **Airborne Virgule** | Uppercut / Wall Launch, then Skill 4 | Changes to a vertical aerial slash |
| **Wolf Fang Miss Tech** | Skill 2 (Aimed slightly away) | Repositions Jun without knocking target away |
| **Combined Finish** | Skill 3 during Awakening 3 | Massive cinematic finishing move |

  <Step num="2" title="The Cascade Transition">

  <Step num="3" title="Post-Cascade Chase">
