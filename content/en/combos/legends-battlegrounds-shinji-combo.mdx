<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Core Mechanics: Lightning Rage & Nunchuck Pressure">

The heart of Shinji's kit is the **Lightning Rage** passive. This mechanic allows you to refresh your most powerful base move, **Flashstep Fury**, by landing other specific abilities. In high-level play, this is what separates a novice from a master. You aren't just using moves; you are managing a resource loop that allows for extended pressure.

| Move Name | Input | Primary Role | Guard Interaction |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Nunchuck Flurry** | 1 | Multi-hit/Reset | Blockable, hits grounded |
| **Body Flicker** | 2 | Displacement | Blockable, fast dash |
| **Lightning Flash** | 3 | Guard Break | **Bypasses Block** |
| **Flashstep Fury** | 4 | Area Pressure | Blockable, multi-target |

<Callout type="warning" title="Important">
Because three out of four moves are blockable, you must use **Lightning Flash** (3) to force the opponent to drop their guard. If you spam Flashstep Fury into a blocking enemy, you will likely be punished during the recovery frames.
</Callout>

Beyond the specials, Shinji's **Electric Wall Sequence** provides a stable environment for damage. When you knock an opponent into a wall, the game triggers a unique animation that pins them, allowing you to follow up with a guaranteed Lightning Flash or a fresh M1 string.
