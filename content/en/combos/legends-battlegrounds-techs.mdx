Once their Q is on cooldown, they are vulnerable to your longest, most damaging combo routes.

| Defensive Tech | Execution | Strategic Result |

<Section icon={Map} title="Character-Specific Technical Mastery">

Each fighter in the roster has unique **legends battlegrounds techs** that define their playstyle. Mastering these character-specific mechanics is essential for climbing the competitive ranks in 2026.

### Jun: The Variant Specialist
Jun relies on "Glimpse Cascade" and "Airborne Virgule" variants. By inputting Virgule Cascade during the animation of Glimpse of Power, Jun performs a unique combined strike that deals significantly more damage than the two moves used separately.

### Shinji: The Reset King
Shinji's core mechanic is "Lightning Rage." When his Flashstep Fury is on cooldown, landing a Nunchuck Flurry will instantly refresh the Flashstep Fury. This allows for a "Double Fury Loop" that can catch multiple opponents in team fights.

### Martial Legend: The Counter Controller
The "Draconic Instinct" passive activates after a successful Triple Kick Counter. This grants a temporary speed boost, allowing the player to side-dash behind the opponent and restart an M1 chain before the opponent can recover from the counter's knockback.

| Character | Difficulty | Primary Tech | Meta Role |

| **Jun** | Advanced | Move Variants | Combo Specialist / Burst |

<Callout type="success" title="Shinji Reset Tip">
To maximize Shinji's damage, always use Flashstep Fury (4) as your first special move. Then, use Nunchuck Flurry (1) to trigger the reset. This ensures you always have a movement tool ready to chase an opponent's escape.

<Section icon={Trophy} title="The Art of the Wall Combo">

Environmental awareness is what separates top-tier players from the rest. Wall combos allow you to deal massive damage by pinning an opponent against map geometry, which prevents them from being knocked away. This is one of the most effective **legends battlegrounds techs** for ending fights quickly.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Positioning the Carry">
    Use a move with high knockback, like Panther Combo or Fang Dance, to push the opponent toward a wall or tree.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Triggering the Sequence">
    Once the opponent hits the wall, immediately follow up with an M1. If the distance is correct, your character will automatically enter a unique "Wall Sequence" animation.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="The Airborne Extension">
    Most wall sequences end with a launcher. For Jun, this is the perfect time to input an Airborne Virgule Cascade. For Shinji, follow up with Body Flicker.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="The Finisher Confirm">
    End the sequence with a guard-breaking move like Tiger Palm or Lightning Flash to ensure the opponent stays down even if they try to block upon landing.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Character | Wall Sequence Effect | Best Follow-up |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Martial Legend** | Multi-hit knee strikes | Dragon Rush (Launch Catch) |
| **Jun** | Upward sword slash | Airborne Virgule Cascade |
| **Shinji** | Electric punch barrage | Body Flicker (Pressure Reset) |

<Callout type="info" title="Map Knowledge">
The best arenas for wall combos are the ones with tight corners or dense foliage. If you are playing as Martial Legend, try to lure opponents toward the edges of the map where your Panther Combo can easily trigger a wall pin.
</Callout>
