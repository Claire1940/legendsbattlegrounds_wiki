Wall combos are not merely automatic; they require precise spatial awareness. If a player activates a launching move too far from a wall, the target will fall before the environmental trigger connects. Conversely, being too close can occasionally cause the physics engine to "clip" the target behind the attacker. Mastering the "sweet spot" distance is what separates casual players from top-tier duelists.

<Section icon={Swords} title="Martial Legend: The Wall Pressure King">

Martial Legend is often considered the most reliable character for executing a **legends battlegrounds wall combo** due to the sheer distance covered by his **Panther Combo**. This move acts as a "carry," pushing the opponent across the map. If the final hit of the Panther Combo occurs near a wall, Martial Legend transitions into a unique sequence involving a knee strike, a heavy body kick, and a spinning tornado kick.

  <Card title="Panther Extension">

To maximize damage, Martial Legend players should not use their **Tiger Palm** before the wall combo. Instead, save the Tiger Palm for the very end of the sequence. Since Tiger Palm hits ragdolled targets and bypasses block, it serves as the perfect "OTG" (Off The Ground) finisher once the opponent is dropped from the wall.

| Move Sequence | Timing Requirement | Purpose |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **3 M1s + Uppercut** | Immediate | Initial launch to prevent early Evasive |
| **Panther Combo** | Mid-air | Carry the target into the wall geometry |
| **Wall Sequence** | Automatic | Main damage dealer and stun lock |
| **Forward Dash + M1** | Frame perfect | Re-restablishes neutral pressure or resets |

<Callout type="warning" title="Evasive Check">
Be careful when using the Panther Combo near a wall if the opponent has their **Q (Evasive)** ready. A skilled player will wait for the wall sequence to begin and then escape, leaving you locked in the animation while they reposition for a counter-attack.

While Martial Legend relies on raw carry distance, Jun utilizes the wall to setup his powerful **Airborne Virgule Cascade** variant. When Jun knocks an opponent into a wall, the resulting launch sends them significantly higher than a standard uppercut. This extra height is critical for triggering the aerial dagger sequence which deals massive cinematic damage.
