<Section icon={Swords} title="Understanding Legends Battlegrounds Finishers">

In the fast-paced world of Roblox arena fighters, **legends battlegrounds finishers** represent the ultimate display of skill and dominance. Unlike standard M1 eliminations that simply ragdoll an opponent, a finisher triggers a high-quality, cinematic animation that locks both the attacker and the victim into a scripted sequence. These moves are not just for show; they provide a satisfying conclusion to a well-executed combo and often showcase the unique martial arts style of the character you are playing.

To trigger these animations, you must understand the health thresholds and move compatibility. A finisher will only activate if the special move deals the killing blow. If an opponent has 5 HP and you hit them with a move that deals 10 HP, the cinematic will trigger. However, if they survive with even 1 HP, the combo continues as normal. This makes "chipping" your opponent down with M1 attacks or low-damage pokes essential before committing your finisher-capable cooldowns.

<Callout type="tip" title="Pro Tip">
If you are struggling to land finishers in public servers, try using the "Downslam" M1 finisher to keep the opponent stationary before launching your final special move.

<Section icon={Target} title="Character-Specific Finisher Lists">

Each fighter in the roster has a specific set of moves capable of triggering a cinematic ending. While the developers at Arts of Vermillion are constantly adding new animations, the current meta revolves around three primary fighters: Jun, Martial Legend, and Shinji. Jun is widely considered the "Finisher King" due to the sheer volume of unique animations tied to his base kit and his "No More Games" awakening.

### Jun's Cinematic Arsenal

Jun’s finishers are divided into base moves and "Move Variants." Variants are triggered by specific environmental conditions or by chaining two moves together, such as using Virgule Cascade during Glimpse of Power.

| Move Name | Type | Guard Property | Finisher Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Glimpse of Power** | Base | Unblockable | A high-speed rush through the target followed by a powerful kick. |
| **Wolf Fang Flash** | Base | Guard Break | A flipping kick into a charged area punch with impact streaks. |
| **Fang Dance** | Base | Guard Break | A long martial arts sequence that carries the opponent forward. |
| **Virgule Cascade** | Base | Blockable | Teleporting dagger strikes ending in a downward area slash. |
| **Airborne Virgule** | Variant | Blockable | An aerial slash sequence triggered after an uppercut launch. |
| **Glimpse Cascade** | Combined | Confirmed | A unique hybrid sequence of dagger slashes and martial strikes. |

### Martial Legend and Shinji Finishers

While Jun focuses on technical variants, Martial Legend and Shinji offer more direct, power-focused eliminations. Shinji’s finishers are particularly notable for their electric visual effects, which were expanded during the August 2025 "Warrior's Grief" update.

| Character | Key Finisher Move | Role | Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Martial Legend** | **Tiger Palm** | Finisher | A guard-breaking strike that hits grounded targets. |
| **Martial Legend** | **Dragon Rush** | Launcher | A forward grab that launches the target into a final blow. |
| **Shinji** | **Lightning Flash** | Guard Bypass | A sequence of flash-step kicks ending in a dropkick. |

<Section icon={Map} title="How to Execute a Finisher: Step-by-Step">

Landing **legends battlegrounds finishers** requires more than just mashing buttons. You need to manage your opponent's "Evasive" (Q-cancel) while ensuring your own cooldowns are ready for the final strike. Follow this reliable route to secure your cinematic eliminations.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Lower the Opponent's Health">
    Engage in neutral play using M1 strings and side dashes. Your goal is to get the opponent below 15% health. Use Martial Legend's **Tiger Palm** or Jun's **Wolf Fang Flash** to chip away at their block.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Bait the Evasive (Q)">
    Opponents will almost always use their ragdoll cancel (Q) when they are low on health. Force them to spend it by using a launcher like an **Uppercut** or **Panther Combo**. Once they dash away, they are vulnerable for several seconds.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Confirm the Final Combo">
    With their evasive on cooldown, land a 3-hit M1 string followed by a **Downslam**. This pins the opponent to the ground, making them a "sitting duck" for your finisher-capable move.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Trigger the Cinematic">
    Input your chosen finisher move (e.g., Jun's **Fang Dance** or Shinji's **Lightning Flash**). If the damage reduces them to zero, the camera will lock, and the cinematic will play automatically.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Combo Mastery">
For the highest success rate, use Jun's **Glimpse Cascade** route (1 then 4). The combined damage of the two moves makes it much easier to hit the zero-health threshold.
</Callout>

</Section>
