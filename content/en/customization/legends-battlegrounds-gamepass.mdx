<Section icon={Shield} title="Combat Mechanics and Recent Update Balancing">

The combat in Legends Battlegrounds is celebrated for its fluidity and depth, drawing heavy inspiration from classic anime fighting games. To keep the gameplay fair and engaging, the developers regularly balance the roster. A recent update introduced significant changes to the **Marshall Legend** class and implemented a highly anticipated anti-cheat/anti-logging system.

The Marshall Legend's ultimate move, **Dragon Blitz**, received a major balance adjustment. Previously, this move disabled the victim's evasive recovery option, making it an inescapable death sentence in high-level play. The developers have removed this evasive-disabling mechanic (a nerf) but compensated by slightly increasing the move's base damage (a buff) to account for the large distance the victim travels during the attack.

| Combat System | Old Behavior | New Behavior (2026) | Competitive Impact |
|--- |--- |--- |--- |
| Dragon Blitz | Disables victim's evasive | Evasive allowed, higher damage | Requires baiting evasives first |
| Combat Logging | No penalty for leaving | 60% damage yields kill credit | Reduces rage-quitting toxicity |
| Map Destruction | Static structures | Real-time building collapse | Creates dynamic cover situations |

Additionally, the new **Combat Log System** ensures that players receive credit for their hard work. If you reduce an opponent's health by 60% or more and they abruptly leave the game to avoid a defeat, you are automatically awarded the kill. This system has drastically reduced toxicity in public lobbies and made leaderboard climbing much more accurate.
