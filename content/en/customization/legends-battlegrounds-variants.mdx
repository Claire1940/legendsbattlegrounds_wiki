One of the most advanced techniques involves the "Wolf Fang Flash Method." This is a specific tech used to extend combos beyond their intended limits by manipulating the move's dash distance.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initiate Backflip">
    Start the Wolf Fang Flash move, but face slightly sideways (roughly 30 degrees) away from your opponent.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Miss the Dash">
    Intentionally miss the initial dash-punch of the move by dashing right to the side of the opponent.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Immediate Follow-up">
    Because you didn't trigger the knockback of the punch, you remain in close proximity to the opponent with your cooldowns resetting, allowing for an immediate M1 restart.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Anti-Teaming and Survival Mechanics">

A unique feature within the **legends battlegrounds variants** system is the dedicated anti-team mechanic. This is specifically designed to level the playing field when a player is outnumbered. When the game detects multiple attackers focusing on a single target, specific variant triggers become available.

| Mechanic | Function | Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Clone Summon** | Summons a shadow clone | Distracts/attacks the second teamer |
| **I-Frame Extensions** | Increases invincibility duration | Allows for safe healing (~5% HP) |
| **AOE Burst** | Expands hitbox of variants | Hits multiple targets simultaneously |

The Anti-Team Clone is a game-changer. It allows the player to focus on a primary target while the clone interrupts the secondary attacker's combos. This is often paired with the **Glimpse of Power + Virgule Cascade Variant**, which provides enough I-frames to mitigate incoming damage from a third party while you execute your main combo.

<Callout type="info" title="Fair Play Feature">
The anti-team mechanic was introduced to ensure that skill remains the deciding factor in battles, preventing toxic "ganging up" behaviors from ruining the gameplay experience.
</Callout>
