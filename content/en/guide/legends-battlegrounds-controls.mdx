### PC Keyboard & Mouse Layout

PC players generally benefit from the highest level of precision, especially when aiming skill shots and executing fast camera turns. The default keybinds utilize the standard WASD cluster for movement, with combat actions mapped closely around it.

| Action | Keybind | Description |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Light Attack | Left Click | Initiates basic M1 combo strings |
| Block | F | Reduces incoming damage from front |
| Dash | Q | Quick directional dodge maneuver |
| Skill 1 | 1 | Activates primary character skill |
| Skill 2 | 2 | Activates secondary character skill |
| Skill 3 | 3 | Activates tertiary character skill |
| Skill 4 | 4 | Activates ultimate character skill |
| Jump | Spacebar | Vertical movement and air recovery |

### Console Controller Layout

Playing with a controller offers comfortable movement tracking thanks to analog sticks. The button mapping focuses on keeping your thumbs on the sticks as much as possible during active combat sequences.

| Action | Controller Input | Description |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Light Attack | X / Square | Primary attack input |
| Block | LT / L2 | Defensive guard stance |
| Dash | RT / R2 | Dodge and evasion |
| Skill 1 | Y / Triangle | First special ability |
| Skill 2 | B / Circle | Second special ability |

### Mobile Touch Layout

<Section icon={Shield} title="Movement and Defense Strategy">

Movement is just as critical as offense in legends battlegrounds. Simply spamming attack keys will quickly result in getting countered and punished by experienced opponents. To survive, you must integrate dashing, perfect blocking, and ragdoll recovery into your active gameplay loop.

Dashing provides brief invincibility frames (i-frames) that allow you to phase through specific high-damage skills. However, dashing consumes stamina, meaning you cannot rely on it constantly without leaving yourself vulnerable.

| Movement Type | Stamina Cost | Tactical Benefit |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Basic Dash | 15 Stamina | Evades skill shots quickly |
| Perfect Block | 0 Stamina | Stuns the attacking opponent |
| Ragdoll Recovery | 50 Stamina | Escapes continuous combo chains |
| Sprinting | 2 per second | Closes distance between targets |

Perfect blocking is executed by pressing the block key right before an enemy attack connects. If timed correctly, you will hear a distinct sound cue, and the attacking player will be briefly staggered, opening them up to a counter-attack.

<Callout type="info" title="Stamina Management">
Keep a close eye on your stamina bar. If you empty your stamina bar while blocking, your guard will break, leaving you stunned and defenseless for several seconds.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Executing Advanced Combat Combos">

To maximize your damage output, you must learn to chain basic attacks with character-specific skills. The standard combo structure involves landing three light attacks (M1), canceling the final knockback animation with a dash or a skill, and continuing the onslaught.

A prime example of this is Jun's combat kit. Jun relies on launching opponents into the air to perform devastating aerial sequences. The signature uppercut is the cornerstone of this playstyle.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initiate the M1 String">
    Approach the target and land exactly three light attacks. Do not execute the fourth attack, as it will knock the opponent too far away to follow up.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Execute the Dash Cancel">
    Immediately after the third hit connects, dash forward to close the minor gap and reset your attack animations.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Trigger the Uppercut">
    Use Jun's designated launcher skill to strike the opponent upward. This requires precise timing to ensure the target does not recover their guard mid-air.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Follow up with Air Combos">
    Jump immediately after the launcher connects to meet the opponent in mid-air, proceeding with aerial light attacks or secondary skills.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Chaining these sequences successfully depends heavily on the damage scaling of your skills. The table below outlines how damage scaling behaves during extended combo strings.

| Combo Stage | Damage Multiplier | Guard Break Potential |
| --- | --- | --- |
| First M1 | 1.0x | Low impact |
| Third M1 | 1.2x | Medium impact |
| Skill Launcher | 1.5x | High impact |
| Aerial Follow-up | 1.8x | Maximum impact |
