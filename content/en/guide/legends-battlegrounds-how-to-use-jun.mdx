<Section icon={Swords} title="Core Movement and Defensive Tech">

The foundation of knowing **legends battlegrounds how to use jun** lies in movement. If you cannot move effectively, you cannot land your combos. The most critical skill is the **side dash**. This isn't just for dodging; it is your primary tool for repositioning mid-fight to find openings in the opponent's guard.

**Side Dash Mastery**
To master the side dash, practice on the training dummy. You should aim to dash to the back of the dummy consistently. This teaches you the exact distance and camera movement required to land a hit immediately after the dash. In a real match, a successful side dash to the opponent's flank almost guarantees a combo start because most players cannot rotate their camera fast enough to block.

| Technique | Input/Action | Purpose |

| **Side Dash** | Shift + A/D | Repositioning, flanking, and combo initiation. |
| **Static Block** | Hold Block | Damage mitigation when abilities are on cooldown. |
| **Air Strafe** | Jump + Direction | Making your approach path harder to predict. |
| **Dash Cancel** | Dash > Move | Stopping a dash early to bait a counter-attack. |

<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Combo Execution">

Executing the perfect combo is the difference between a mid-tier player and a legend. Jun's kit allows for "move stacking," where you use specific knock-up or knock-down mechanics to keep the opponent in a stun lock.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Bait the Evasive">
    Before starting a full combo, hit the opponent once or twice and back off. You want them to waste their **evasive maneuver**. If you commit to a long combo while they have evasive ready, they will break out and punish you during your end-lag.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The 5 M1 Opener">
    Use the advanced **uppercut with front dash** technique. This allows you to land five M1 hits instead of the standard four, giving you a significant damage advantage right at the start of the engagement.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Move Stacking">
    After your initial M1 string, transition into an **uppercut**. Time your next move perfectly as the opponent is descending. This "stacking" allows you to chain your special abilities together without giving the opponent a frame to recover.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Down Slam Finisher">
    End your sequence with a **down slam**. This forces the opponent into a grounded state, giving you enough time to reset your positioning or wait for your shortest cooldowns to return.
  </Step>
</Steps>
