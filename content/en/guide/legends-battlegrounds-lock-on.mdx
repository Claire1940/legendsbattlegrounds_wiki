<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Lock-On Configuration">

Configuring your camera settings properly is essential before entering competitive matches. Follow this step-by-step routing to optimize your interface, eliminate input delay, and set up your camera offset for maximum combat efficiency.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Enable Shift Lock Switch">
    Open the Roblox game menu by pressing Escape, navigate to the Settings tab, and locate the Shift Lock Switch option. Ensure this is toggled to On. This allows you to press the Left Shift key during gameplay to lock your camera perspective.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Adjust Camera Offset Options">
    Navigate to the in-game settings menu provided by Arts of Vermillion. Locate the shift-lock offset sliders introduced in the October 2024 update. Adjust the horizontal offset so your stickman sits slightly to the left of the screen, providing a clearer view of incoming frontal attacks.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Toggle Photosensitive and Performance Modes">
    If you experience frame drops during intense visual effects, toggle on the Photosensitive Mode in the game settings. This reduces flash intensity and stabilizes your frame rate, ensuring your camera tracking remains smooth during rapid lightning or windstorm animations.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Configure Mobile Touch-Lock">
    For players on mobile or tablet devices, tap the dedicated lock icon on the right side of the screen. This activates the touch-lock system, which replicates the PC Shift Lock functionality by centering your attacks on the nearest target within your visual arc.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Warning: Avoid Default Sensitivity Overload">
Setting your camera sensitivity too high while locked on can cause extreme screen shaking during multi-hit moves like Shinji's Flashstep Fury or Jun's Virgule Cascade. Keep your camera sensitivity between 0.6 and 1.2 for optimal tracking.
</Callout>

</Section>
