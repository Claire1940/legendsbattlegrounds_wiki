<Section icon={Swords} title="Base Moveset and Lightning Rage Passive">

Understanding Shinji's core mechanics is essential before mastering the **Legends Battlegrounds Draco Meteor**. His base kit revolves around high-speed strikes and electrical zaps that stun and displace enemies. A unique feature of Shinji is his 5-hit M1 chain; while technically four inputs, the third M1 consists of two nunchuck strikes, providing better hit-stun and pressure than standard 4-hit characters.

The core of his kit is the **Lightning Rage** passive. This mechanic creates a synergy between his offensive tools and his mobility. When Flashstep Fury is on cooldown, using Nunchuck Flurry or Body Flicker will reset it, though this reset itself has a 50-second internal cooldown indicated by a blue bar.

| Attack Name | Damage | Cooldown | Utility |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **M1 Chain** | 15% | N/A | 5-hit combo ending in a spinning hook kick |
| **Nunchuck Flurry** | 17% | 30s | Multi-hit upwards strike/Vertical mobility |
| **Body Flicker** | 13% | 20s | Electric blitz through enemies/Dash extension |
| **Wall Combo** | 16% | N/A | Contextual grab that pins enemies to objects |

<Callout type="tip" title="Pro Combat Tip">
Nunchuck Flurry can hit ragdolled players. Use this to catch opponents who have already used their ragdoll cancel to extend your combo into the air.
</Callout>

### The Lightning Rage Rotation
To maximize Shinji's effectiveness, players must master the cooldown reset. The standard rotation involves using Flashstep Fury to engage, then immediately following up with Nunchuck Flurry to reset the Flashstep cooldown. This allows for an immediate second use or a quick escape if the initial engagement fails.

<Section icon={Target} title="Mastering the Awakening: Draco Meteor & Ultimates">

The Awakening state transforms Shinji from a technical fighter into a cinematic powerhouse. The **Legends Battlegrounds Draco Meteor** is the crowning achievement of this state. This move is a direct homage to the "Honored One" sequence from Jujutsu Kaisen, emphasizing Shinji's status as a dominant force on the battlefield.

Shinji's ultimate progression is split into three distinct phases, each more damaging than the last. Unlike base moves, these ultimate abilities often involve cutscenes where the user is protected from damage, though they can be canceled if the user is hit during the brief startup window.

| Ultimate Move | Damage | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Warrior's Grief** | 29% | Cinematic entry with electric AOE wave and minor stun |
| **Iron Onslaught** | 83%/66% | Multi-angle high-speed punch barrage and skull shatter |
| **Draco Meteor** | Massive | Third Awakening move; the ultimate "Honored One" reference |

<Callout type="warning" title="Vulnerability Window">
All of Shinji's ultimate moves, including the path to Draco Meteor, can be canceled if you are hit before the cinematic begins. Always ensure your opponent is stunned or out of evasives before initiating.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Strategic Matchups and Anti-Teaming">

Shinji is widely considered the "Teamer Killer" in **Legends Battlegrounds**. Most of his moves, including Nunchuck Flurry and Body Flicker, have hitboxes capable of catching multiple players simultaneously. This makes him exceptionally dangerous in 2v1 or 3v1 situations where opponents are grouped together.

However, Shinji has clear weaknesses. Almost his entire moveset can be blocked. A disciplined player who holds their ground can mitigate most of Shinji's damage and punish his long cooldowns.
