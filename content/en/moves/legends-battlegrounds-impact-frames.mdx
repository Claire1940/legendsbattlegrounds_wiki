<Section icon={Sparkles} title="The Mechanics of Legends Battlegrounds Impact Frames">

Understanding the underlying mechanics of **legends battlegrounds impact frames** is the difference between a casual player and a competitive veteran. In high-speed combat, every animation is divided into three distinct phases: startup frames, active (impact) frames, and recovery (endlag) frames. Impact frames are the specific window where your character's weapon or limb possesses a "hitbox" capable of interacting with an opponent's "hurtbox."

In Legends Battlegrounds, mastering these windows allows you to "frame trap" opponents, forcing them into situations where they cannot block or dash away. For instance, moves like Jun's *Glimpse of Power* have very fast startup frames, making them difficult to react to, followed by unblockable impact frames that guarantee a stun.

| Frame Phase | Description | Gameplay Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Startup** | The beginning of an animation before the hit occurs | Determines how easily a move can be interrupted |
| **Active/Impact** | The window where the hitbox is dangerous | The moment damage and stuns are applied to the enemy |
| **Recovery** | The animation finishing after the hit or miss | Leaves the player vulnerable to counter-attacks |

<Callout type="tip" title="Pro Timing Tip">
To maximize your efficiency, always look for moves with low recovery frames (endlag). If you miss a move like *Wolf Fang Flash*, the low endlag allows you to recover quickly enough to block or dash before the opponent can punish your mistake.
</Callout>
