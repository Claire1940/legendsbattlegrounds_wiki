<Section icon={Target} title="Mastering the Piercing Tempest Sequence">

The **legends battlegrounds piercing tempest** is not just a single attack but a multi-stage cinematic sequence. Success with this move depends on your positioning and the opponent's current defensive state. Because the initial knife throw can be blocked, professional players rarely use it as a raw opener. Instead, they confirm it after a knockdown or during a wall-splat.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initiate the Transformation">
    Ensure your awakening meter is at 100%. Create distance using a side dash or a knockback move like Wolf Fang Flash, then press **G**. The transformation creates a small shockwave that can protect you from immediate rushdown.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Confirm the Opening">
    Do not throw the knives immediately. Use **Phantom Sever** (Move 2) to close the gap or force the opponent to spend their evasive (Q). Once the opponent is in hitstun or recovering from a dash, trigger Piercing Tempest.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="The Teleport Barrage">
    Upon a successful knife hit, Jun will automatically begin the teleport sequence. During this phase, you are invulnerable to outside interference, making it an excellent tool for team fights where you need to isolate a single target.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Execute the Combined Finisher">
    For maximum damage, press **4 (One Inch Punch)** while the Piercing Tempest animation is active. This replaces the standard ending with a devastating close-range strike that deals significantly higher burst damage.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="Pro Strategy">
If the opponent blocks the initial knives, do not panic. The cooldown for Piercing Tempest is moderate, and you can still use Savage Fangs to maintain pressure while waiting for a reset.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Matchup Analysis and Strategy">

In the current meta of Legends Battlegrounds, Jun occupies a unique space. While he can out-damage Martial Legend, he must be wary of Shinji's superior mobility and reset mechanics.

**Against Martial Legend:**
Martial Legend players will look for a **Triple Kick Counter**. Do not use Fang Dance or Piercing Tempest predictably. Bait the counter with a side dash, then punish the recovery with Glimpse of Power.

**Against Shinji:**
Shinji is the fastest character in the game. His **Flashstep Fury** can interrupt your knife throw. Focus on blocking his nunchuck flurries and use your unblockable Glimpse of Power to stop his momentum. Once Shinji's "Lightning Rage" passive is on cooldown, he is vulnerable to a full Piercing Tempest sequence.

| Opponent | Jun's Advantage | Danger Zone |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Martial Legend** | Unblockable starters | High-damage counters |
| **Shinji** | Stronger burst potential | Faster chase and resets |
| **Mirror Match** | Better timing wins | Getting caught in a tempest first |
