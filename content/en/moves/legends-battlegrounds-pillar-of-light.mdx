<Section icon={Map} title="Core Controls and Combat Fundamentals">

Before you can master advanced techniques like the **legends battlegrounds pillar of light** mechanics, you must internalize the basic control scheme. The game supports PC, Mobile, Tablet, and Console, but the competitive meta is primarily driven by precision keyboard inputs on PC.

| Input | Action | Strategic Use |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Left Click** | Light Attack (M1) | The foundation of every combo; stop at 3 hits for launchers. |
| **Q Key** | Dash / Side Dash | Used for repositioning or closing distance after a knockback. |
| **Q (Ragdolled)** | Evasive / Cancel | Your only escape from a combo; has a long cooldown. |
| **F Key** | Block | Nullifies most M1 damage; can be bypassed by guard breaks. |
| **G Key** | Awakening | Triggers the transformation and ultimate abilities. |
| **B Key** | Emote Wheel | Used for social interaction or "taunting" after a finisher. |

The "Combat Loop" in Legends Battlegrounds is a game of chicken. You want to bait your opponent into spending their **Q (Evasive)** early. Once their ragdoll cancel is on cooldown, they are vulnerable to a full 100-to-0 combo string.

<Callout type="info" title="Pro Tip: Block Management">
Holding **F** is safe against standard M1s, but moves like Martial Legend's **Tiger Palm** or Jun's **Glimpse of Power** will break your guard. If you see these animations start, use a side-dash instead of holding block.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step: Your First Combat Session">

To effectively use the **legends battlegrounds pillar of light** and dominate your matches, follow this structured progression path designed for 2026's competitive environment.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Select Martial Legend">
    Start with Martial Legend. His kit is the most forgiving, featuring a reliable counter and a guard-breaking palm strike that hits grounded targets.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Practice the 3-M1 Rule">
    Never finish a full 4-hit M1 chain unless you want to knock the opponent away. Stop at the 3rd hit, then use an **Uppercut** (Space + M1) to keep them in the air.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Master the Side Dash">
    Dashing straight forward into a blocking opponent is a death sentence. Practice dashing to the left or right (Side Dash) to get behind their guard for an M1 starter.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Confirm the Awakening">
    Wait for your bar to turn gold. Once you land a **Dragon Rush** or **Panther Combo**, activate your Awakening to unleash the "Pillar of Light" and finish the fight.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="Evasive Discipline">
Do not use your **Q Evasive** on the very first hit you take. Save it for when the opponent commits to a long-cooldown move like **Fang Dance** or **Dragon Rush**. This wastes their ability and gives you an opening to counter-attack.
</Callout>

</Section>
