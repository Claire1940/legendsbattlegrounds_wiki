<Section icon={Swords} title="Unlocking Legends Battlegrounds Savage Fangs">

The combat sandbox of Roblox stickman PvP reaches its peak when players transition into their awakened states. Among these, Jun's "No More Games" transformation is highly feared due to its sheer offensive pressure. At the center of this awakened kit is the **legends battlegrounds savage fangs** ability, a high-impact, single-target burst move that can turn the tide of any duel. 

To access this ability, you must first select Jun and fill your awakening meter completely. This is achieved by engaging in active combat, landing light attacks, and successfully executing base-kit skills. Once the meter is full, pressing `G` triggers a dramatic transition where Jun draws his sword, replacing his standard daggers and unlocking a brand-new set of four cinematic abilities. Savage Fangs is assigned to the first skill slot (`Key 1`) in this state.

When activated, Savage Fangs initiates a rapid sequence of sword slashes. If the initial strike connects, the move transitions into a long, cinematic grab animation that deals massive damage while locking the opponent in place. This locking mechanism is highly valuable in team fights, as it prevents the target from escaping while your teammates position themselves for follow-up pressure.

<Callout type="warning" title="Guard Interaction">
The initial grab of Savage Fangs can be blocked if executed raw in neutral play. To maximize your success rate, always confirm the strike during an active hitstun window or immediately after an opponent spends their ragdoll escape.
</Callout>

In the fast-paced environment of Roblox arena fighters, understanding the exact startup frames and grab range of your ultimate moves is the difference between securing an elimination and being punished. Savage Fangs has a brief forward-dashing startup, meaning you can use it to catch opponents who are attempting to back-dash or create space. However, missing this move leaves Jun highly vulnerable to a counter-attack, making disciplined execution essential.

<Section icon={Target} title="Savage Fangs Combo Routes & Execution">

To consistently land the **legends battlegrounds savage fangs** ability in competitive matches, you must implement structured combo routes. Mashing your skills randomly will result in blocked attacks and wasted cooldowns. Instead, follow a disciplined sequence that prioritizes hit confirmation and baiting the opponent's defensive options.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Build the Awakening Meter">
    Engage in close-range skirmishes using your M1 light attack chain. Stop at three hits, execute an uppercut or downslam, and follow up with Glimpse of Power or Fang Dance to generate meter safely.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Create Space for Activation">
    Once your meter is full, do not activate awakening immediately in front of your opponent. Use a knockback move like Wolf Fang Flash or a wall combo to create distance, then press G to transform without being interrupted.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Bait the Opponent's Evasive">
    Initiate your awakened pressure with Phantom Sever or basic M1 strikes. Watch for the opponent's ragdoll cancel (Q). Once they spend this critical defensive tool, they are completely vulnerable.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Confirm Savage Fangs">
    Execute a three-hit M1 chain followed by a downslam. While the opponent is recovering from the knockdown, immediately activate Savage Fangs to guarantee the grab connects.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Execute the Combined Finisher">
    After Savage Fangs completes, follow up with Piercing Tempest. Press One Inch Punch during the multi-angle teleport barrage to trigger Jun's ultimate combined cinematic ending.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Executing these steps in order ensures that you do not waste your powerful awakened cooldowns. In high-level PvP, players will actively look to interrupt your awakening animation or block your ultimate moves. By creating space before pressing `G` and confirming your hits before activating Savage Fangs, you minimize the risk of counter-play.

The Savage Burst is perfect for quickly punishing a mistake, while the Full Awakening route is designed to completely deplete an opponent's health bar when all your cooldowns are available. Practice these routes in private servers with cooldowns disabled to build the muscle memory required for live matches.

<Section icon={Shield} title="Matchup Strategies & Counter-Play">

Succeeding in high-level PvP requires more than just knowing your own combos; you must also understand how to deploy your skills against specific opponents. Every character in the game has unique defensive tools and offensive tempos that you must actively counter. When playing as Jun, your goal is to use your mobility and unblockable options to disrupt your opponent's rhythm.

When facing Martial Legend, you must play patiently. Martial Legend players will actively look to bait your Fang Dance or Savage Fangs with their Triple Kick Counter. To counter this, use empty dashes to bait their counter stance, wait for the active frames to end, and then punish their high recovery with a confirmed M1 string. Avoid throwing raw attacks in neutral, as a successful counter will grant Martial Legend a speed boost via Draconic Instinct, allowing them to turn the tide of the exchange.

| Opponent Fighter | Key Threat to Avoid | Counter-Play Strategy | Savage Fangs Setup |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Martial Legend** | Triple Kick Counter / Tiger Palm | Bait the counter with empty dashes; block Tiger Palm | Force a missed counter, then confirm Savage Fangs |
| **Jun (Mirror)** | Glimpse of Power / Savage Fangs | Keep distance; track their evasive; block raw Fang Dance | Wait for them to miss Glimpse, then strike |
| **Shinji** | Flashstep Fury Reset / Lightning Flash | Block the multi-hit barrage; dodge Lightning Flash | Catch them mid-charge of Lightning Rampage |

Against Shinji, your primary defense is disciplined blocking. Most of Shinji's base special moves, including Nunchuck Flurry and Flashstep Fury, can be completely blocked. However, you must remain vigilant against Lightning Flash, as it bypasses your block entirely. Use your side dashes to avoid Lightning Flash, then immediately close the distance before Shinji can reset his cooldowns using his Lightning Rage passive.

<Callout type="warning" title="Counter Alert">
Never activate Savage Fangs if Martial Legend is standing still in neutral. They are likely holding their Triple Kick Counter, which will completely absorb your ultimate attack and initiate a devastating retaliation sequence.
</Callout>

In mirror matches against another Jun player, the fight is decided by who manages their cooldowns more effectively. Avoid spending all four of your base moves during a single unconfirmed exchange. Keep Glimpse of Power ready to interrupt their approaches, and preserve your Wolf Fang Flash as a mobility tool to escape their wall combos. The player who baits the other's evasive first will almost always secure the victory.
