<Section icon={Target} title="Mastering the 2026 Combat Mechanics">

With the introduction of character-specific variants, the combat loop has become significantly more complex. The **legends battlegrounds update log** highlights the "Glimpse Cascade" and "Airborne Virgule" as the new standards for high-level Jun play. These moves require precise input timing—activating a secondary skill during the animation of the first. This "Move Variant" system is expected to expand to Martial Legend and Shinji in future 2026 patches.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Confirm the Opener">
    Start with an M1 string (usually 3 hits) followed by an uppercut or downslam. Do not commit high-cooldown specials until you see the hit confirm.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Check Evasive Usage">
    Watch the opponent's "Q" usage. If they spend their ragdoll cancel early, you are free to commit to a full wall-carry route or a cinematic awakening move.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Trigger Move Variants">
    For Jun, input Virgule Cascade during Glimpse of Power. For Shinji, use Nunchuck Flurry to reset Flashstep Fury via the Lightning Rage passive.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Environmental Finish">
    Direct the final knockback toward a wall to trigger the character-specific wall sequence for bonus damage and a favorable reset.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="Pro Strategy">
Always keep one movement tool (like Shinji's Body Flicker or Jun's Wolf Fang Flash) off cooldown. Using all four moves in one string leaves you defenseless against a counter-dash.
</Callout>

</Section>
